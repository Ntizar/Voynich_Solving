What Was Done In The Final Weeks

  • Rewrote the documentation to separate structure from reading.
  • Built a shared blind benchmark across three input representations: STA stems, STA tokens, and EVA tokens.
  • Diagnosed the historical recipe corpus for overlap, ingredient concentration, and poor train support.
  • Created a disciplined corpus-expansion workflow with candidate scoring and source tracking.
  • Mined open external pharmacopoeia witnesses from Amsterdam editions and tested them in a parallel augmented corpus.

What Holds Up

  • The recipe section shows anomalous structure compatible with a database or structured notation system.
  • The text does not behave like a normal polyalphabetic or homophonic cipher.
  • The structural signal is robust across validation-style checks and corpus comparison.

What Does Not Hold Up

  • Specific stem -> ingredient readings do not beat trivial baselines in blind evaluation.
  • The manual v7 table remains contaminated by test exposure and circular target selection.
  • External recipe expansion improved some scores, but did not produce a decisive semantic win.
